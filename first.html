Formula 1 Pit Stop Predictor

Analyze the factors affecting pit stop strategies in Formula 1 races. Consider weather impact, track condition, engine performance, and lap ranges to optimize your race strategy.

Weather Impact:

  • Hot Climate: High tire degradation, so pit stops might need to be scheduled earlier.
  • Cold Climate: Lower tire degradation, allowing for delayed pit stops.

Track Condition:

  • Dry Track: Less tire wear, so pit stops might be spread out evenly.
  • Wet Track: Higher chance of tire change due to rain, leading to more frequent pit stops.

Engine Suppliers:

  • Mercedes AMG F1: Generally reliable, may require fewer pit stops.
  • Red Bull Ford Powertrains: Strong performance but may have higher tire wear.
  • Ferrari: Moderate tire wear characteristics.
  • Honda F1: Higher reliability but conservative on pit strategy.
  • Audi: Balanced performance.

Lap Ranges:

  • Early laps (1-15): Usually require an aggressive strategy, possibly fewer stops if fuel load is high.
  • Mid-race laps (16-60): Standard degradation phases where tire changes are crucial.
  • Late laps (61-75): Focus on tire preservation or aggressive overtakes.
